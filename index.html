Chittagong Traditional Mezban :

Author:Shahid Khan

The mejbani is a social event in the Chittagong region. Mejbani is the arrangement of hospitality, hospitality or banquet for guests. A public feast entered on any occasion. mejbani are organised on the occasion of death or death of loved ones, birth or birthday, any success of themselves, starting a new business, entering a new home, and naming a newborn.Invitations to the mejbani are open to all. When a rich person organises a feast in the countryside, the invitation to the feast is conveyed to different villages and neighbourhoods by different people.
In Chittagong, rich and poor organise feasts on various occasions as much as possible. White rice is served with three or four types of curry such as beef, nala kanji and kalai or boot dal. Its cuisine is characteristic, such as (1) salted meat cooked with pepper and spices; (2) less salty with beef tube, start cooking with spices or kanji, which is known as kanji; (3) A type of dal is cooked by peeling the maskalai and peeling it in a dhemki or machine.

Arabian Food

Saudi Arab Traditional Kabsa :

Author:Sourav Khan

Kabsa is an intrinsic part of the region’s culinary heritage as it is arguably the best representation of traditional Arabic cuisine. Kabsa truly does justice to its popularity and pride of place as Saudi Arabia’s national dish. And that surely makes it a must-try Saudi specialty.
As one of the most popular and traditional Arabic dishes and of course being the national dish of Saudi Arabia, Kabsa is widely available across the country from Jeddah to Riyadh. You can definitely sample this dish at fine dine restaurants and food courts. However the most authentic experience to try the traditional Kabsa would be at the home of a local. Make friends with your local guide and see if you are lucky enough to get invited to dine with his family.

Chinese Food

Chinese Traditional Meat Recepie :

Author:Salman Ali Khan

Ma Po Tofu is one of the most famous dishes in Chuan Cuisine (Sichuan food) with a history of more than 100 years. It consists of beancurd along with some minced meat (pork or beef) in a spicy sauce. The sauce is made from fermented black beans and chili paste (douban/douchi).
The inventor of Ma Po tofu was from Chengdu, Sichuan province. She was a grandma whose surname was Chen. It is said that Chen’s face was very pockmarked. In Chinese, ma means ‘pockmarked’ and 'po’ means grandma, and so people called the dish she made Ma Po ('Pockmarked Granny’) tofu. Her spicy and fragrant dish later became a highly popular item. It was introduced to Japan and also became a popular dish there. Read more about how to cook Ma Po Tofu.
